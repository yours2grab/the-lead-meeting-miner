## Client Notes

### Focus and Priorities

- Virgil opened with: "If we're going to do both at the same time, we will fail both." Team debated selling custom automations vs building the newsletter.
- Victor agreed the audience needs to come first regardless of what they sell later.

**Decision:** Newsletter growth is priority #1. Don't split focus with a separate automation service. Sell through back-channels when opportunities come.

### Revenue Model

- Base layer: Sucana SaaS subscription (analytics + AI chat)
- Enterprise layer: custom automations built on top of Sucana
- Victor: "The service is the enterprise plan. Get Sucana customized. That's it."
- Pricing idea for custom work: start at $10K, scale to $20K per client

**Decision:** Position custom work as "enterprise plan." Sucana is always the base. Services create new tools that get incorporated back into the platform.

### Client Portal and Reporting

- Victor built a client login portal in one afternoon. Clients log in, see automated weekly reports.
- He's using Virgil's ad-reporting skill but with client-specific context: "For this kind of funnel, these are the metrics that matter."
- Virgil: "The client-facing thing is what agencies want."
- Victor: "Vinod can plug this into Sucana in one day."

**Decision:** Victor tests the portal with his own agency clients first. Then the team builds it into Sucana.

### Context Engine

- Victor demonstrated how Claude Code stores client context across conversations: team info, funnel type, competitor data, admin docs. All auto-organized.
- Virgil: "It's not an AI wrapper anymore. It becomes a living entity."
- Victor: "People won't go to another tool once they have years of knowledge inside."

**Decision:** Build this into Sucana in stages: data analysis first, then context layer, then enhanced reporting.
